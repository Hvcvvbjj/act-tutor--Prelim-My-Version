# Enhanced ACT blueprint used by Scout ACT

Scout ACT targets the enhanced ACT structure in effect for current administrations. It does not use the legacy four-section Composite calculation.

## Official displayed structure

| Section | Displayed questions | Scored questions | Standard time | Current reporting-category mix |
| --- | ---: | ---: | ---: | --- |
| English | 50 | 40 | 35 minutes | Production of Writing 38–43%; Knowledge of Language 18–23%; Conventions of Standard English 38–43% |
| Math | 45 | 41 | 50 minutes | Preparing for Higher Math 80%; Integrating Essential Skills 20%; Modeling at least 20% and overlapping |
| Reading | 36 | 27 | 40 minutes | Key Ideas and Details 44–52%; Craft and Structure 26–33%; Integration of Knowledge and Ideas 19–26% |
| Science (optional) | 40 | 34 | 40 minutes | Interpretation of Data 38–50%; Scientific Investigation 18–32%; Evaluating Scientific Arguments and Models with Evidence 25–35% |
| Writing (optional) | 1 essay | 1 essay | 40 minutes | Ideas and Analysis; Development and Support; Organization; Language Use and Conventions |

The enhanced Composite is the rounded average of English, Math, and Reading. Science and Writing remain optional and do not enter this Composite.

## Scout ACT half-length baseline

The no-score path uses half of each displayed core section, rounding the odd Math count upward:

| Section | Scout questions | Target time | Format |
| --- | ---: | ---: | --- |
| English | 25 | 18 minutes | Five original revision passages with five questions each |
| Math | 23 | 25 minutes | Original four-choice standalone and contextual problems |
| Reading | 18 | 20 minutes | Three original passages with six questions each |
| Total | 66 | About 63 minutes | Autosaved, resumable, and scored only after submission |

Every question has exactly four answer choices, matching enhanced Math's reduction from five answer options to four. English and Reading use passage-grouped workspaces. The form deliberately includes more medium and hard items than the old rapid form so a high-scoring learner cannot establish a ceiling by answering only elementary isolated questions.

## Skill evidence

The current 12-skill evidence layer sits beneath the official reporting categories:

- English: sentence boundaries; concision and redundancy; punctuation and commas; logical transitions.
- Math: linear equations; functions and modeling; ratios and percent; geometry and measurement.
- Reading: central ideas and details; supported inference; textual evidence and details; author purpose and structure.

Each skill receives four to seven questions in the half-length form. Reporting categories preserve the official section-level vocabulary, while skill labels drive lessons and practice selection.

## Accuracy boundary

The blueprint validates structure, distribution, original-content metadata, unique choices, and valid answer keys. It does not make the form psychometrically equivalent to an official ACT administration. Results must remain labeled **estimated practice score ranges** until an independent content review, a larger calibration sample, item analysis, and empirical score conversion are complete.
